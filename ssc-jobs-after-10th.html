1. Introduction

Securing a position under the Central Government remains one of the most reliable career pathways in India. The Staff Selection Commission (SSC) serves as the premier constitutional recruitment vehicle, designing annual entry frameworks for aspirants who have concluded their 10th standard (Matriculation) boards.

Instead of waiting to achieve structural college degrees, matriculate applicants can directly enter the federal administrative machinery. These roles provide strong financial baselines, institutional job protection, standard healthcare privileges, and definitive structured departmental routes for climbing higher into executive cadres.

Quick Strategic Summary

SSC exams after the 10th standard unlock entry-level Group C jobs across ministries, central secretariats, and paramilitary commands. The primary core frameworks are SSC MTS (Multi-Tasking Staff) for civilian admin support and SSC GD (General Duty) Constable for security services. In the 2026 cycle, MTS CBT was held 4–20 February 2026, while GD Constable CBT ran across multiple shifts from 27 April to 30 May 2026.

Can I Get an SSC Job Directly After 10th?

Yes. SSC conducts recruitment exams like SSC MTS and SSC GD Constable for candidates who have completed Class 10 from a recognized board.

3. Eligibility Criteria

To clear the Initial Screening Filters of the Commission, candidates must perfectly fulfill specified structural criteria covering education benchmarks and structural age constraints:

  • 🎓 Education: Must have explicitly cleared the Matriculation (10th Class) Examination or an equivalent accredited training block from a recognized state or central board.
  • ⏳ Age Limit (MTS): 18 to 25 years as on 1 January 2026 (born between 02-01-2001 and 01-01-2008).
  • 🎖️ Age Limit (Havaldar, CBIC/CBN): 18 to 27 years, plus a mandatory Physical Standard Test (PST) and Physical Efficiency Test (PET).
  • ⚔️ Age Limit (GD Constable): Strictly bounded between 18 to 23 years as on 1 January 2026 (born between 02-01-2003 and 01-01-2008).
  • 🛡️ Statutory Relaxations: Age criteria are extended by 3 years for OBC applicants and 5 years for SC/ST categories according to federal constitutional provisions; higher relaxation applies for PwBD and Ex-Servicemen candidates.

4. Selection Process

The standard evaluation mechanics require passing through multiple verification stages to confirm technical and physical aptitude.

Stage 1: Computer Based Examination (CBE)
A unified, competitive online screening exam consisting of multiple-choice questions designed to measure raw intelligence, quantitative capacity, and language baselines.
Stage 2: Physical Standard & Efficiency Validation (PET/PST)
Mandatory explicitly for Havaldar and GD Constable roles. Evaluates structural height, expansion matrices, and stamina through timed run tracking.
Stage 3: Document Verification & Medical Inspection
Final credential checks verifying state records and qualification proofs, paired with a standard medical review to certify operational fitness.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can a 10th-pass student get a permanent job in the Central Government?

Yes. The Staff Selection Commission (SSC) conducts national-level competitive exams like SSC MTS and SSC GD Constable explicitly for candidates who have passed their 10th standard board examinations, offering permanent Group C government service jobs.

What is the minimum and maximum age limit for SSC 10th pass jobs in 2026?

For SSC MTS the age bracket is 18 to 25 years (18 to 27 years for Havaldar posts under CBIC/CBN), and for SSC GD Constable it is 18 to 23 years, both reckoned as on 1 January 2026. Age relaxations apply for reserved category applicants (OBC, SC, ST, PwBD, and Ex-Servicemen) as per standard Central Government guidelines.

Is there a physical fitness test for all SSC jobs after the 10th standard?

No. A Physical Efficiency Test (PET) and Physical Standard Test (PST) are mandatory only for operational defense forces like the SSC GD Constable and the Havaldar post. Non-technical administrative desks like SSC MTS strictly prioritize computer-based tests followed directly by document checks.

Is there any application fee for SSC MTS and GD Constable 2026?

Yes. General, OBC, and EWS candidates pay ₹100 for SSC MTS. SC, ST, Women, PwBD, and Ex-Servicemen candidates are fully exempt from the fee. SSC GD Constable follows a similar exemption structure.

Is there a limit on the number of attempts for SSC MTS or GD?

No. There is no attempt limit for SSC MTS or SSC GD Constable — candidates can apply every year as long as they meet the age and educational eligibility criteria for that cycle.

Which pays more after 10th — SSC MTS or SSC GD Constable?

SSC GD Constable pays more. It sits at Pay Level-3 (in-hand ≈ ₹36,000–₹40,000/month) compared to SSC MTS at Pay Level-1 (in-hand ≈ ₹22,000–₹30,000/month), since GD Constable is an operational security role.
